# Figure Notes

## 01_u5_trend_by_income.png
This figure shows the average under-5 mortality rate over time by World Bank income group. It supports the main predictor side of the research question by showing how child mortality has changed from 1990 to 2023.

## 02_adol_trend_by_income.png
This figure shows the average adolescent fertility rate over time by income group. It supports the outcome side of the research question by showing whether adolescent fertility has declined over the same period.

## 03_life_exp_trend_by_income.png
This figure shows life expectancy trends by income group. Life expectancy is used as a broader health and population dynamics indicator in the project.

## 04_gdp_trend_by_income.png
This figure shows GDP per capita trends by income group. It provides economic context for differences across countries and income groups.

## 05_variable_distributions.png
This figure shows the distributions of the key variables used in the analysis: under-5 mortality, adolescent fertility, life expectancy, and GDP per capita.

## 06_u5_vs_adol_scatter.png
This figure shows the same-year relationship between under-5 mortality and adolescent fertility. It is useful as an exploratory plot before looking at the lagged relationship.

## 07_u5_vs_adol_faceted_by_income.png
This figure shows the relationship between under-5 mortality and adolescent fertility separately by income group. It helps show whether the relationship varies across income levels.

## 08_lagged_u5_vs_adol_scatter.png
This figure shows the relationship between under-5 mortality five years earlier and current adolescent fertility. This is the figure most directly connected to the research question and the lagged regression idea.
